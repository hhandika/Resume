Heru Handika

I travel across islands and mountains in Southeast Asia to find answers for these questions: what is a species? how did past geological events shape biodiversity we know today? How do species adapt to extreme environment?

My work combines rigorous fieldwork to remote places, artwork through phytography and graphic design, and computer work using available scientific tools, so that we have improved knowledge on species diversity. Currently, I am a PhD student in Esselstyn's Lab at the Museum of Natural Science and the Department of Biological Sciences, Louisiana State University, USA.
